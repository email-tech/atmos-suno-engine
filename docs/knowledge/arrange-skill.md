# Arrangement & Instrumentation Skill Specification

## Overview

This skill governs how the app turns musical ideas into full arrangements: deciding which instruments play which parts, controlling texture, register, dynamics, and shaping the overall sonic architecture of a piece.

## Core Subskills

- Instrument roles: understanding typical functions of drums, bass, harmony instruments, melody instruments, and texture layers.
- Register and density: managing low, mid, and high ranges and the number of simultaneous parts.
- Texture and layering: building, thinning, and varying the arrangement over time.
- Orchestration: assigning lines and harmonies to different instruments or sections.
- Dynamics and energy flow: using volume, articulation, and arrangement changes to control intensity.

## Key Concepts

- Arranging is about clarity and contrast: choosing which elements are foreground, midground, and background at any moment.
- Register separation and complementary timbres help prevent muddiness and masking.
- Changes in instrumentation, texture, and dynamics create section differences and emotional contour.

## Tools, Techniques, and Workflows

- Arrangement sketching: start with a simple lead + harmony + rhythm, then add or remove layers per section.
- Orchestration workflow: consider range, timbre, and technical capabilities of instruments when assigning parts.
- Energy mapping: chart intensity across sections (e.g. intro, verse, pre-chorus, chorus, bridge) and plan arrangement changes to match.

## Learning Progression

### Beginner

- Build clear verse–chorus arrangements with distinct instrument roles.
- Avoid overcrowding the mix by limiting simultaneous parts and focusing on the lead.

### Intermediate

- Use dynamic build-ups, drops, and textural changes to enhance song structure.
- Orchestrate simple lines for small ensembles or band settings.

### Advanced

- Design complex arrangements for large ensembles, orchestras, or dense productions while preserving clarity.
- Use advanced orchestration techniques (doubling, divisi, counterlines, colouristic effects).

## Common Mistakes and Misconceptions

- Making every part busy, leading to clutter and masking.
- Ignoring register, causing important lines to compete in the same range.
- Treating arrangement as an afterthought rather than a primary compositional tool.

## Cross-Links to Other Skills

- Musical Foundations: informs harmonic and rhythmic decisions that underpin arrangements.
- Songwriting & Composition: provides core material (melody, lyrics, progressions) to arrange.
- Production & Mixing: relies on good arrangements for efficient mixing; arrangement choices affect frequency balance and spatial design.
