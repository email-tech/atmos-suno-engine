# Production, Recording, Editing, Mixing & Basic Mastering Skill Specification

## Overview

This skill defines how the app approaches the technical and creative side of turning musical ideas into finished audio: setting up sessions, recording, editing, sound design, mixing, and basic mastering.

## Core Subskills

- Session setup and workflow: project structure, track naming, routing, and file management.
- Recording fundamentals: signal flow, gain staging, microphone choice and placement.
- Editing: comping, timing correction, tuning, cleaning noise, and preparing tracks for mixing.
- Sound design and processing: synthesis, sampling, EQ, compression, saturation, reverb, delay, modulation.
- Mixing: balance, panning, frequency management, dynamics, space, and bus processing.
- Basic mastering: overall tonal shaping, loudness, and final quality control.

## Key Concepts

- Good production starts with solid source material: arrangement, performance, and recording quality.
- Gain staging and routing provide a clean, controlled foundation for all processing.
- Mixing is primarily about clarity and focus: ensuring the most important elements are audible and emotionally impactful.
- Mastering polishes and standardises tracks for distribution but cannot fix fundamental mix or arrangement issues.

## Tools, Techniques, and Workflows

- Production workflow: composition/arrangement  recording  editing  mixing  basic mastering.
- Recording workflow: plan inputs, set levels, monitor, capture multiple takes, and back up sessions.
- Mixing workflow: set static balance, correct major tonal issues, then refine with compression, EQ, and effects; reference against trusted tracks.
- Mastering workflow: check translation across playback systems, adjust overall EQ and dynamics, set loudness targets appropriate for platforms.

## Learning Progression

### Beginner

- Set up simple projects, record basic tracks, and apply minimal processing to achieve clean and balanced sound.

### Intermediate

- Use routing, bus processing, and more advanced editing to handle multi-track sessions.
- Build consistent mixing habits and reference-based decision-making.

### Advanced

- Design complex soundscapes, handle large and demanding sessions, and tailor mastering approaches to genre and distribution format.

## Common Mistakes and Misconceptions

- Over-reliance on plugins instead of focusing on source quality and arrangement.
- Ignoring gain staging, leading to distortion or noisy signals.
- Over-processing (too much EQ/compression) that removes natural dynamics and tone.

## Cross-Links to Other Skills

- Arrangement & Instrumentation: a clear arrangement simplifies production and mixing.
- Musical Foundations: informs tonal and rhythmic decisions in processing.
- AI Music Creation: AI-generated stems still require human production, editing, and mixing for professional results.
