# Songwriting & Composition Skill Specification

## Overview

This skill defines how the app approaches songwriting and composition: generating ideas, crafting lyrics, designing melodies and hooks, structuring songs, and shaping emotional narratives across different genres.

## Core Subskills

- Concept and theme development: choosing topics, emotional arcs, and central ideas.
- Lyric writing: rhythm of language, rhyme, imagery, prosody, and clarity.
- Melody and hooks: contour, repetition, variation, and singability.
- Song structure: verse, chorus, bridge, pre-chorus, intro/outro, and alternative forms.
- Harmony and progression design: choosing chords that support the melody and mood.
- Workflow and revision: drafting, feedback, rewriting, and finishing songs.

## Key Concepts

- Effective songs balance familiarity and surprise: recognisable structures and patterns with distinctive twists.
- Lyrics and melody should support each other; prosody aligns natural speech stress with musical emphasis.
- Hooks are short, memorable musical or lyrical ideas that anchor the listener and define the song.
- Song forms provide frameworks for pacing tension and release across sections.

## Tools, Techniques, and Workflows

- Idea capture: free-writing, voice memos, chord loops, and simple prompts to generate concepts.
- Lyric development workflow: brainstorm, outline sections, draft verses/chorus, then refine for clarity and impact.
- Melody writing workflow: start over a simple progression, experiment with contour and rhythm, then refine for singability and hook strength.
- Structure design: map emotional arc, choose section order, and align lyrical content with musical intensity.

## Learning Progression

### Beginner

- Write simple verse–chorus songs with clear topics and repetitive hooks.
- Use basic progressions (I–V–vi–IV, I–IV–V) and straightforward melodies.
- Practice finishing short songs rather than perfecting fragments.

### Intermediate

- Explore bridges, pre-choruses, and more varied forms.
- Use more nuanced lyric techniques (metaphor, contrast, internal rhyme) while maintaining clarity.
- Develop melodies that work across different harmonic contexts and registers.

### Advanced

- Integrate advanced harmony, modulation, and complex rhythms into songs without losing accessibility.
- Design concept albums or multi-song arcs with recurring motifs.
- Adapt songwriting across genres (pop, rock, EDM, hip hop, folk, film scoring) while preserving core emotional intent.

## Common Mistakes and Misconceptions

- Relying solely on inspiration instead of treating songwriting as a craft with repeatable processes.
- Overcomplicating lyrics or harmony, making songs hard to remember or sing.
- Ignoring structure, leading to songs that feel static or meandering.

## Cross-Links to Other Skills

- Musical Foundations: provides the harmonic and rhythmic vocabulary for progressions and melodies.
- Arrangement & Instrumentation: turns song ideas into full arrangements with effective instrumentation and dynamics.
- Production & Mixing: shapes how songs are presented sonically and how hooks are emphasised.
- AI Music Creation: can generate drafts, but this skill defines how to evaluate and refine them.
